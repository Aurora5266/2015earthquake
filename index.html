Nepal Earthquake 2015: A Dark Day in History

On April 25, 2015, a devastating earthquake with a magnitude of 7.8 struck Nepal, with its epicenter near the village of Barpak in the Gorkha district. It was the worst natural disaster to hit the country in over 80 years. The earthquake caused massive destruction, killing nearly 9,000 people and injuring more than 22,000. Thousands of homes, historical monuments, temples, and infrastructure were reduced to rubble, especially in the Kathmandu Valley.

A powerful aftershock of magnitude 7.3 occurred on May 12, worsening the damage and further traumatizing the population. Remote villages were hardest hit, where access to aid was delayed due to difficult terrain.In the aftermath, international aid poured in, and rescue and relief operations were launched across the country. Despite the tragedy, the disaster also showcased the resilience and unity of the Nepali people.
